Season Trends

League-level trends (environment, recurring patterns)
- High-variance scoring environment: the league regularly produces multiple 100+ team totals and occasional 140–160 and 150+ team weeks. Evidence includes multiple team outputs above 140 in prior weeks and frequent single-player blowups.
- Close games/upsets are common: several matches have been decided by 1–4 points. Bench/inactive decisions and D/ST/kicker performances have produced many upsets.
- D/ST and kickers matter: D/STs and kickers have produced substantial positive deltas vs projections on many occasions (examples: Vikings D/ST 32.0, Browns D/ST 24.0, Steelers D/ST 22.0; kicker weeks by Jason Myers, Cameron Dicker, Eddy Pineiro).
- Bench/inactive management is a persistent issue: repeated occurrences of 20+ point bench performances that, if started, would have swung outcomes (examples: Daniel Jones 29.48 bench; Bijan Robinson 35.8 bench; Trevor Lawrence 33.96 bench).
- Owner behavior patterns: smart behaviors include starting high-upside RBs and streaming D/ST/kickers; risky behaviors include leaving obvious high-scorers on the bench and failing to adjust for inactives/BYEs.
